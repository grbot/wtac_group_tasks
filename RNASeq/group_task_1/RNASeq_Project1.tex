    \section{Title: Analysis of the transcriptional landscape in a knock out
parasite
(1)}\label{title-analysis-of-the-transcriptional-landscape-in-a-knock-out-parasite-1}

\textit{Plasmodium falciparum} is the causative agent of the most
dangerous form of malaria in humans. The reference genome for \textit{P.
falciparum strain 3D7} was determined and published about 15 years ago
(Gardener et al., 2002). Since then, the genomes of several other
species of \textit{Plasmodium} that infect humans or animals have been
elucidated.

Malaria is widespread in tropical and subtropical regions, including
parts of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. Each year, there are
approximately 350--500 million cases of malaria killing more than one
million people, the majority of whom are young children in sub-Saharan
Africa.

As working within human is not feasible, mouse models are used like
\textit{Plasmodium berghei} or \textit{Plasmodium chabaudi}. They can easily
be maintained in the lab and mirror specific phenotypes as found in
human malaria.

A colleague of yours knocked out a gene (\textbf{PBANKA\_KO}) in the
rodent malaria parasite \textbf{\textit{Plasmodium berghei}}. Over the
last three months, she generated different biological replicates of the
wild type (\textbf{WT}) and the knock out (\textbf{KO}) to understand
differences between both lines.

She needs to find out what the function of the gene is to finalize her
grant application and she has a meeting with her boss to go through the
\textbf{FINAL} results this afternoon!!!

Unfortunately, on the way to work, she broke both her hands cycling and
cannot do the analysis!!!! Can you help her out?

You can find the data for this project on your VM:

\textbf{/home/manager/course\_data/group\_projects/RNASeq\_1}

Your job is to understand the implication of the knock out of the gene.

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  What influence does it have?\\
\item
  What is the name of the knock out gene?
\item
  How did you determine those?
\end{itemize}

Use your knowledge from the RNA-Seq module. It might be worth giving the
tasks of genome mapping/visualisation and the transcriptome
mapping/analysis to different team members so that you can run them at
the same time. \textit{Careful, some of the needed files might not be
present...}

Useful sites:

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  www.plasmodb.org - Gene information and GO enrichment (ask your
  instructor if/when you get to this point)
\item
  www.genedb.org
\item
  ftp://ftp.sanger.ac.uk/pub/project/pathogens/gff3/CURRENT/ - for
  annotation -- look for Pberghei\textit{gff3}
\end{itemize}

Can you save your poor colleagues and save the grant....?
